Dr. Kalpana Chawla

" First Indian Woman In Space , Pioneer of the stars "

Reaching for the Stars: The Remarkable Journey of Kalpana Chawla

  • 1962 : Born on March 17, 1962 in Karnal, India. Youngest of the 4 children.

  • 1982 : Engineering Graduate - Punjab Engineering College, India.

  • 1984 : Immigrated to the United States to pursue her dreams.

  • 1988 : Master's in Aerospace Engineering - University of Texas

  • 1994 : Becomes a U.S. citizen.

  • 1997 : Selected by NASA for astronaut training.

  • 1997 : Made her first journey into space aboard Space Shuttle Columbia.

  • 2001 : Achieved the rank of Mission Specialist on her second spaceflight.

  • 2003 : Conducted important scientific research during her final mission.

  • 2003 : Inspired millions as a role model for women in STEM.

  • Kalpana Chawla tragically lost her life on February 1st, 2003, in the Shuttle Columbia disaster, marking a somber moment in the history of space exploration.

"Kalpana Chawla's life was short but her achievements were great. Her courage to break barriers inspired many." ~ Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam
